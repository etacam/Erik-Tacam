About Me

My name is Erik Tacam and I am a data science and political science enthusiast. I was born and raised in New York City and attended Brooklyn Technical High School. I am currently studying at Colby College, a top private liberal arts college in the Northeastern part of the United States. At Colby, I study computer science and government, along with a minor in mathematics. With the combination of my interests, I inspire to have a career in technology policy.

On campus, I have been part of the leadership team for two new clubs - the Colby Lions Club and the Colby Cyber Security Club. In 2018, I became the co-founder and vice-president of the Colby Lions Club. The purpose of the club is to be an on-campus branch of the Waterville Lions Club in order to better coordinate volunteering activities for the Colby community to participate in Waterville. As vice-president, I was able to coordinate a fundraising event that yielded $1,300 to sponsor a 14-year old girl's education in Zimbabwe. In the same year, I became a board member of the Colby Cyber Security Club. The purpose of the club is to teach cybersecurity lessons to the Colby community. As a board member, I helped to coordinate hackathons and conferences for the club members to partake in and taught monthly cybersecurity lessons in club meetings.

When I am not busy doing schoolwork, I like to travel, hike, and camp with my friends in the beautiful landscape of Maine. Maine's landscape is unmatched and offers a lot of hiking and camping opportunities. During the winter season, I like to go alpine skiing at Sugarloaf Mountain. Moreover, I like to play soccer with my friends as well as watch movies and documentaries.

Experience

Besides having a few leadership roles for clubs on campus, I've been a Collections Management Assistant, Operations Assistant, student mentor, and academic mentor.

As a Collections Management Assistant at Colby College Libraries, I am responsible for ensuring accurate coordinating and tracking in the exchange of over 500,000 available books and materials between the Colby libraries and delivering exceptional service to the Colby library patrons.

As an Operations Assistant for the Colby College Mathematics and Statistics Department, I managed department operations by ensuring collaborative work and learning spaces are ready for use by students and that faculty can focus on research and teaching responsibilities. I was also trusted to carryout time sensitive and confidential tasks to support administrative staff.

I am a student mentor for a fourth-grade student at Winslow Elementary School where I am responsible for helping the student improve his literacy skills by fostering an adult friendship.

As an academic mentor for two incoming first-year students, I was responsible for helping to facilitate their transitions into a new academic life at Colby College.

Travels

Over the past year, I have been fortunate enough to travel around the world to strengthen my academic background. In January 2019, I traveled to Uganda as part of a one-month study abroad program where I had the opportunity to explore international and local models of development. Throughout the month, I visited various development projects and met with aid and development professionals to understand challenges development actors face. For the fall semester of my junior year, I went to study abroad in Copenhagen, Denmark. Throughout the semester, I learned about humaniarian law and armed conflict. The focus of the program was to have an understanding of the international law regulation of armed conflict - including humanitarian law, human rights law and international criminal law. The knowledge was achieved by studying a number of contemporary case studies with a specific emphasis on Kosovo. As part of the program, I went to Kosovo for a week to learn more about the post-conflict humanitarian crisis that the nation is currently facing after becoming an independent nation in 2008.

Besides traveling around the world to strengthen my academic background, I traveled to new cities to explore new cultures, architectual styles, and foods. By far, my favorite city that I visited in Europe was Prague. The architectural style of Old Europe, the food, and the people were amazing and distinguishably unique from the rest of Europe. I would definitely recommend that anyone should visit Prague if given the opportunity to do so. Besides Prague, I visited other beautiful cities such as Barcelona, Lisbon, and London.
